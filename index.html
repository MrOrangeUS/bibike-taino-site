About & Vision

Bibi’ké Taíno draws its inspiration from the Taíno peoples of the Caribbean and their deep reverence for Atabey, the Mother of Waters and goddess of fertility. In Taíno cosmology, Atabey is the mother of Yaya and is associated with life-giving waters and fertility【24366099096727†L612-L623】. The Taíno saw the island itself as a living organism, populated by deities and spirits who influenced every aspect of life【24366099096727†L612-L623】. Another ancestor spirit, Itiba Cahubaba—the Great Bleeding Mother—gave birth to the universe, and her sons created the ocean and land【605843869432207†L20-L24】.

Some petroglyphs are interpreted as anthropomorphic representations of Atabey; she is seen as a consciousness manifesting through natural forces such as storms and fertility cycles【551353384645949†L149-L193】. Hurricanes (hurakan) are regarded as the fury of Guabancex, an aspect of Atabey, reflecting the cyclical nature of destruction and renewal【551353384645949†L149-L193】. To the Taíno, nature is alive and intimately connected to human wellbeing—reconnecting to Atabey is vital for cultural and ecological balance【551353384645949†L221-L230】. Our community embodies this worldview by building regenerative, off‑grid neighborhoods that honor the earth while embracing cutting-edge decentralized technologies.

Bibi’ké Token & DAO

Our community’s economy runs on the Bibi’ké Token, a fixed-supply utility token that powers transactions, voting rights, and rewards. Holders can purchase land deeds, participate in governance, and receive incentives for sustainable contributions. A portion of the supply is allocated to a community treasury for infrastructure, cultural initiatives, and resilience-building.

Governance is handled through the Bibi’ké DAO. Every adult resident holding tokens can propose and vote on budgets, development plans, and cultural events. Youth councils contribute ideas that require adult endorsement. The DAO ensures transparency by recording all votes and transactions on-chain and publishing decisions via a public dashboard.

NFT Property Deeds

Each parcel of land in Bibi’ké Taíno is represented by an NFT deed. By minting an NFT, you gain ownership rights to a plot and membership in the community. NFTs are minted through a smart contract; once purchased, your deed is recorded immutably on the blockchain. The deed NFT grants access to community facilities, a share of resource outputs, and a voice in the DAO.

Minting occurs in phases, with priority given to early supporters and residents. A user-friendly dApp will guide you through connecting your wallet, selecting the quantity of deeds, and completing the transaction.

Government Permits & Compliance

Sustainability goes hand in hand with compliance. Our developments are fully permitted and adhere to environmental, building, and zoning regulations. We work closely with local authorities to secure off‑grid construction permits, water harvesting licenses, and renewable energy approvals. Our environmental assessments and impact studies ensure that developments protect ecosystems, respect wetlands, and preserve cultural sites.

Ongoing monitoring and transparent reporting keep our community in good standing. We proactively engage with regulators and neighbors, sharing plans and adjusting designs based on feedback. By leading with integrity and transparency, we set the standard for responsible regenerative development.

Buyer Information

Purchasing a deed in Bibi’ké Taíno means more than acquiring land—it’s an invitation to co‑create a culture. Buyers gain access to high‑performance eco‑homes equipped with renewable energy systems, rainwater harvesting, and integrated permaculture gardens. Homes are designed to be net‑positive, producing more energy than they consume.

To buy, connect your wallet, choose an available parcel, and mint the deed NFT. Once ownership is recorded, our team assists with site selection, construction, and community onboarding. Deed holders share responsibility for governance and upkeep while enjoying shared amenities such as community kitchens, makerspaces, health centers, and schools.
