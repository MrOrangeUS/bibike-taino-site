About & Vision

Bibi’ké Taíno is more than a neighbourhood – it’s a movement. Inspired by the Taíno people and the energy of Atabey (Mother Earth), our mission is to build regenerative communities that honour ancestral wisdom while embracing modern, decentralised technologies.

We design self‑sufficient eco‑villages where homes harvest their own water and energy, food is grown locally, and decision making occurs transparently through a Decentralised Autonomous Organisation (DAO). Every resident is a steward of the land and has a voice in how the community evolves.

Bibi’ké Token & DAO

The Bibi’ké Token is the lifeblood of our economy and governance. It acts as a medium of exchange within the community and grants holders the right to vote on proposals through the Bibi’ké DAO.

  • Fixed supply: To protect long‑term value, the token supply is capped. A portion is allocated to the community treasury for infrastructure and cultural programs.
  • Utility: Purchase property deeds, participate in governance, and earn rewards for contributions to energy production, food growing, and community service.
  • Governance: Every adult resident holding tokens can vote on proposals. Proposals covering budgets, infrastructure upgrades, and cultural events require a supermajority to pass.

Visit our whitepaper (coming soon) for detailed tokenomics, distribution, and vesting schedules.

NFT Property Deeds

Ownership of land in a Bibi’ké Taíno community is represented by a unique non‑fungible token (NFT) deed. Minting a deed grants you the right to occupy and steward a specific plot within our neighbourhood.

  • Digital & legal: Each NFT deed corresponds to a legally compliant land contract, stored on‑chain and backed by traditional legal agreements. This ensures your ownership is recognised both digitally and in the real world.
  • Resale & transfer: You may transfer your deed to another party according to community guidelines and applicable regulations.
  • Minting process: To acquire a deed, connect your wallet, obtain Bibi’ké Tokens, and mint during the public sale. Quantities are limited to ensure balanced community growth.

Detailed instructions on how to mint and manage your deed will be available on our minting portal once the sale begins.

Government Permits & Compliance

Bibi’ké Taíno operates within the legal framework of each jurisdiction where we build. Our team works closely with authorities to secure all necessary permits and approvals. These include zoning and land use approvals, environmental impact assessments, building permits, and infrastructure permits for water and power systems. Our approach emphasises transparency and collaboration to ensure compliance and longevity.

  • Environmental compliance: We conduct full impact studies, adhering to environmental protection laws and integrating renewable technologies that reduce our footprint.
  • Land use & zoning: Each community site is approved for residential and agricultural use. Agreements with municipal or county authorities confirm that our off‑grid infrastructures meet or exceed building codes.
  • Legal recognition of NFTs: Our legal team collaborates with regulators to ensure NFT deeds are recognised alongside traditional land titles, providing you with a secure and enforceable property right.

We share updates about permits and compliance openly. Investors and buyers can review documentation in our due diligence portal (coming soon). If you have questions about regulatory status, contact us.
